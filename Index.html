About Energy Audit

What is Energy Audit?

An energy audit is a systematic examination of energy usage in a building or facility to identify opportunities for energy conservation and efficiency improvements. It involves detailed analysis of equipment, operational patterns, and consumption data.

Importance of Energy Conservation:

  • Reduces operational and maintenance costs
  • Minimizes environmental impact and carbon footprint
  • Promotes sustainable development practices
  • Improves equipment lifespan and performance
  • Enhances comfort and working conditions
  • Supports compliance with energy regulations

Project Objectives:

  • Identify current energy consumption patterns
  • Detect inefficiencies in electrical systems
  • Quantify energy losses across different equipment
  • Calculate potential cost savings
  • Provide actionable recommendations for optimization
  • Establish baseline for future improvements

Results & Recommendations

🔴 Energy Losses Identified:

  • Inefficient HVAC System: 28% higher consumption than industry standard
  • Outdated Lighting: 15% energy waste due to use of halogen bulbs
  • Poor Insulation: 22% heat loss through windows and doors
  • Phantom Loads: 8% standby power consumption from inactive equipment
  • Equipment Inefficiency: 12% performance degradation due to lack of maintenance

✅ Recommendations for Energy Savings:

  • LED Retrofit Program: Replace all lights with LED → 60% lighting savings
  • HVAC Optimization: Upgrade to high-efficiency units → 30% HVAC savings
  • Insulation Upgrade: Improve window sealing and insulation → 20% cooling savings
  • Smart Controls: Install occupancy sensors and thermostats → 15% overall savings
  • Regular Maintenance: Preventive upkeep schedule → 10% efficiency improvement
